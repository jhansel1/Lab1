Monthly Customers on the CTA Red Line
"The Backbone of Chicago"

This web mapping application illustrates the monthly customers at each of the 33 stations on the CTA Red Line.


The Chicago Transit Authority (CTA) opened the train line running between the North/Clybourn and Roosevelt stations on October 17, 1943. Over the decades the line was increased to take travelers and commuters from the northside to the southside.


The CTA adopted color-coded nomenclature for the rail routes in 1993, and this line has since forth been called the "Red Line". The Red traverses many different neighborhoods, from the most affluent to some of the most disadvantaged. Millions of people ride the train annually, including sports fans to Cubs, White Sox and Bears games.
